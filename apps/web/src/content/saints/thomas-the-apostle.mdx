## Overview

Saint Thomas the Apostle, also known as Didymus (meaning "Twin") or Mar Thoma, was one of the Twelve Apostles chosen by Jesus Christ. While famously nicknamed "Doubting Thomas" due to his initial skepticism regarding the Resurrection, his encounter with the Risen Christ led to one of the most profound confessions of faith in Scripture: *"My Lord and my God!"* 

According to ancient tradition, Thomas traveled as far as India around AD 52, establishing Christian communities along the Malabar Coast (Kerala) before suffering martyrdom near Chennai.

## Call to Discipleship and Character

Thomas was personally chosen by Jesus to be one of the Twelve. Although the Gospels do not describe the specific moment of his call, he followed Jesus throughout His public ministry. 

Thomas is portrayed in the Gospels as an honest, courageous, and deeply loyal disciple who desired certainty. When Jesus resolved to return to Judea—where His life was threatened—Thomas courageously rallied the other Apostles: *"Let us also go, that we may die with him."*

## The Last Supper and the Resurrection

During the Last Supper, Thomas showed his desire for clear understanding when he asked: *"Lord, we do not know where you are going. How can we know the way?"* Jesus answered with the foundational truth: *"I am the way, and the truth, and the life."*

Thomas is most famous for his reaction to the news of the Resurrection. Absent when Jesus first appeared, he declared he would not believe unless he saw the nail marks in Jesus' hands and put his hand into His pierced side. Eight days later, Jesus appeared and invited Thomas to touch His wounds. Overwhelmed, Thomas declared: *"My Lord and my God!"*

## Mission to India

Following Pentecost, Thomas set out to spread the Gospel to the East. While early traditions place him in Parthia, Persia, and Mesopotamia, the most cherished and historically significant tradition is his mission to India. 

According to the St. Thomas Christian tradition, he arrived on the Malabar Coast (modern-day Kerala) in AD 52. He established seven and a half churches (Christian communities) along the coast, laying the foundations for the ancient Syrian Christian heritage of India.

## Martyrdom

Thomas eventually traveled to the Coromandel Coast (eastern India) to continue his missionary work. His preaching and conversions angered local rulers and priests. 

Around AD 72, while praying on St. Thomas Mount near present-day Chennai (Mylapore), he was attacked by soldiers and martyred, pierced to death with spears. He died a courageous witness to the Gospel he had carried thousands of miles.

## Relics and Legacy

Saint Thomas's tomb is traditionally located at the San Thome Basilica in Chennai, India, which remains a major pilgrimage destination. Portions of his relics were later translated to Ortona, Italy. 

In iconography, he is depicted touching Christ's side, holding a spear (representing his martyrdom), or carrying a carpenter's square (representing his patronage of builders and architects).
