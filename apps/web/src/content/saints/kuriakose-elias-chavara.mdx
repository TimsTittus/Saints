## Overview

Saint Kuriakose Elias Chavara was a pioneer of education, social reform, and monastic life in 19th-century India. As the co-founder of the **Carmelites of Mary Immaculate (CMI)**, the first indigenous religious congregation for men in India, he revolutionized the Syro-Malabar Church and broader society in Kerala. He was canonized in 2014 by Pope Francis.

## Early Life and Priesthood

Kuriakose was born on February 10, 1805, in Kainakary, Kerala, into a devout Catholic family. Showing academic promise and deep piety, he entered the seminary at Pallipuram and was ordained a priest in 1829. 

## Religious Foundations

Recognizing the need for structured religious communities within India, Father Chavara co-founded the Congregation of the Carmelites of Mary Immaculate (CMI) in 1831 at Mannanam. He also co-founded the Congregation of the Mother of Carmel (CMC) in 1866, the first native congregation for women, establishing a path for religious women in India.

## Social Reforms and Education

Chavara was a visionary educationist who believed that education was the key to social progress. As Vicar General of the Syro-Malabar Church, he issued a historic decree mandating that every church under his jurisdiction must build a school (*Pallikkoodam*) open to children of all classes and castes. He also founded India's first Catholic printing press at Mannanam, which published spiritual texts, journals, and educational material.

## Spiritual Legacy and Canonization

Father Chavara spent his final years in Koonammavu, where he passed away on January 3, 1871. He was beatified by Pope John Paul II in Kottayam, India, on February 8, 1986, alongside Saint Alphonsa. He was canonized by Pope Francis at the Vatican on November 23, 2014, honoring his immense legacy as a spiritual leader and champion of social equity.
