## Overview

Saint Alphonsa Muttathupadathu, affectionately known as the **Passion Flower of India**, is a celebrated Syro-Malabar Catholic nun and the first native Indian female to be canonized as a saint. Throughout her brief life, she endured severe physical ailments, offering her immense suffering to God in spiritual union. Today, she is revered globally as a powerful intercessor, especially for those suffering from illnesses.

## Early Life and Vocations

Alphonsa was born Anna Muttathupadathu on August 19, 1910, in Kudamalloor, Kerala, India. She was raised by her maternal aunt, who envisioned a comfortable, married life for her. However, from a young age, Anna felt a deep calling to consecrate herself to religious life. To escape an arranged marriage, she deliberately stepped into a burning pit of chaff, severely burning her feet. This act of determination paved the way for her to enter the Franciscan Clarist Congregation.

## Religious Life and Severe Illness

She received the postulant's habit in 1928, taking the religious name Alphonsa in honor of Saint Alphonsus Liguori. In 1930, she received the religious habit and commenced her spiritual formation. 

Her life in the convent was marked by severe health issues. She suffered from hemorrhages, skin ailments, and intense physical pain. In 1939, a severe attack of double pneumonia left her extremely weakened. Through all these tribulations, she maintained a joyful demeanor and declared that suffering was a gift from God, writing: *"I do not suffer even a single moment without joy."*

## Death and Pilgrimage

Saint Alphonsa passed away peacefully on July 28, 1946, at the young age of 35 in Bharananganam, Kerala. Her funeral was small, but soon after her burial at St. Mary's Church, school children and locals began reporting miracles through her intercession. Her tomb quickly turned into a major pilgrimage center, drawing millions of believers of all faiths.

## Canonization

She was beatified along with Saint Kuriakose Elias Chavara by Pope John Paul II during his historic visit to India in 1986. On October 12, 2008, Pope Benedict XVI canonized Saint Alphonsa, making her the first woman of Indian origin to be raised to the honors of the altar.
