## Overview

Saint Mark the Evangelist, also known as John Mark, was the author of the Gospel of Mark, widely considered the earliest of the four canonical Gospels. A close companion of Saint Peter and a missionary associate of Saint Paul and Saint Barnabas, Mark played a foundational role in the early Church. 

According to ancient tradition, Mark founded the Church of Alexandria in Egypt, serving as its first bishop before suffering a martyr's death around AD 68. He is traditionally symbolized by the winged lion.

## Family and Early Life

Mark was born in the early 1st century, traditionally in Jerusalem. His mother, Mary of Jerusalem, owned a prominent home in the city that served as an essential meeting place for the early Christian community. 

Following Saint Peter’s miraculous escape from Herod's prison, it was to Mary’s house that he immediately went to find the gathered disciples (Acts 12:12). This upbringing placed Mark at the heart of the apostolic community from its very beginning.

## Companion to Peter and Paul

Mark's missionary journey began in earnest when he accompanied his cousin Barnabas and Saint Paul on Paul's first missionary journey. However, Mark withdrew early and returned to Jerusalem, which later caused a disagreement between Paul and Barnabas. Years later, Paul and Mark reconciled, with Paul noting that Mark was *"helpful to me in my ministry"* (2 Timothy 4:11). 

Mark was also a close companion and spiritual son of Saint Peter, who affectionately called him *"my son Mark"* (1 Peter 5:13). Early Church fathers testify that Mark’s Gospel was written in Rome, directly recording Peter's eyewitness preaching and testimony.

## Author of the Gospel of Mark

Mark is credited with writing the second Gospel, characterized by its fast-paced, urgent narrative. He frequently uses the word *"immediately"* to propel the story forward, focusing heavily on Jesus' actions, authority, and calls to discipleship. 

The Gospel emphasizes Christ's suffering, presenting Him as the Suffering Servant to encourage Roman Christians facing persecution.

## Founding of the Church of Alexandria and Martyrdom

Ancient tradition holds that after preaching in Italy, Mark traveled to Alexandria, Egypt. There he preached the Gospel, converted many, and established the Church of Alexandria, becoming its first bishop. Under his guidance, Alexandria became a major intellectual and cultural center for early Christianity. 

Around AD 68, during a pagan festival, Mark was arrested by local authorities. He was tied with ropes and dragged through the streets of Alexandria until he died.

## Symbols and Legacy

In Christian iconography, Saint Mark is symbolized by a **winged lion**, derived from the vision of the four living creatures in Ezekiel and Revelation. The lion represents the voice crying out in the wilderness at the beginning of his Gospel, as well as the courage, strength, and royal dignity of Christ. 

His relics are primarily enshrined in the magnificent St. Mark’s Basilica in Venice, Italy.
