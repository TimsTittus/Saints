## Overview

Saint James the Less, also known as James, Son of Alphaeus, was one of the Twelve Apostles chosen by Jesus Christ. He is traditionally identified by many in the Western Church as the first Bishop of Jerusalem, playing a central role in guiding the early Christian community and presiding over the Council of Jerusalem. 

Catholic tradition also associates him with the authorship of the Epistle of James, which famously teaches that *"faith by itself, if it has no works, is dead."* He was martyred in Jerusalem around AD 62 after refusing to deny Christ.

## Why "the Less"?

James is referred to as "the Less" (or "the Younger") primarily to distinguish him from the other Apostle named James—Saint James the Greater (son of Zebedee). The title likely refers to him being younger in age, smaller in physical height, or called later to follow Jesus. It does not imply that he was in any way less holy or important than the other Apostle.

## Call to Discipleship and Role among the Twelve

Chosen by Jesus to be one of the Twelve Apostles, James followed Christ faithfully throughout His public ministry. Although the Gospels do not detail the specific moment of his calling, he is listed among the Apostles in the Synoptic Gospels and the Acts of the Apostles. He was present during major events, including the Ascension and the descent of the Holy Spirit at Pentecost, serving as a pillar of the emerging Church.

## Bishop of Jerusalem and the Council

James became the leader of the Christian community in Jerusalem, traditionally recognized as its first bishop. In this role, he presided over the Council of Jerusalem (Acts 15), a pivotal meeting where the Apostles resolved how Gentile converts would be integrated into the Church. 

James delivered the final judgment, deciding that Gentile believers were saved by grace and did not need to follow the full Mosaic law, while gently requesting they respect certain traditions to maintain unity with Jewish Christians.

## The Letter of James

Christian tradition identifies James as the author of the Epistle of James. Writing to the scattered Christian communities, his letter is highly practical, focusing on the active application of Christian faith. 

He famously emphasizes that true faith must manifest in good works: *"Be doers of the word, and not hearers only."* His writings outline key themes such as maintaining control over the tongue, seeking wisdom from above, praying with faith, and showing care for the poor.

## Martyrdom

According to early Church historians like Hegesippus, James's preaching in Jerusalem angered the Jewish authorities. He was taken to the pinnacle of the Temple and ordered to deny Jesus before the crowd. 

Instead, James loudly proclaimed Jesus as the Son of God. He was thrown down from the Temple heights. Surviving the fall, he knelt to pray for his attackers, saying, *"Lord, forgive them, for they know not what they do."* He was then stoned and finally beaten to death with a fuller's club.
