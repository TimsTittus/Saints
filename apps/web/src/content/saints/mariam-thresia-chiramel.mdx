## Overview

Saint Mariam Thresia Chiramel Mankidiyan was an Indian Catholic religious sister and the founder of the **Congregation of the Holy Family (CHF)**. She dedicated her life to visiting, assisting, and praying for families in distress, particularly the poor and marginalized. Renowned for her mystical experiences and stigmata, she was canonized by Pope Francis in 2019.

## Early Life and Mystical Call

Thresia was born on April 26, 1876, in Puthenchira, Kerala, to a noble family that had fallen into poverty. From a very young age, she experienced deep mystical visions, including apparitions of the Holy Family. She secretly took a vow of perpetual virginity and dedicated herself to prayer, fasting, and service to the sick.

## Mission to Families

Unlike many religious sisters of her era who lived in cloister, Mariam Thresia felt called to active apostolic work in the community. Accompanied by three companions, she visited homes to help the sick, the dying, and families experiencing domestic trials. 

In 1914, she formally founded the Congregation of the Holy Family to carry out this mission of family apostolate, establishing schools, orphanages, and hostels.

## Spiritual Battles and Stigmata

Throughout her life, Mariam Thresia experienced the stigmata (the wounds of Christ) and underwent intense spiritual and physical warfare. She offered these trials for the conversion of sinners and the sanctification of families.

## Death and Canonization

Mariam Thresia passed away on June 8, 1926, in Kuzhikkattussery, Kerala. She was beatified on April 9, 2000, by Pope John Paul II and canonized on October 13, 2019, by Pope Francis. Her shrine at Kuzhikkattussery remains a focal point for families seeking intercession and blessing.
