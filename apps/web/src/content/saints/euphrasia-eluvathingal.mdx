## Overview

Saint Euphrasia Eluvathingal, affectionately referred to as the **Praying Mother** (*Prarthana Amma*), was an Indian Carmelite nun who lived a life of profound contemplation and union with God. Bound to her convent walls, she became a lighthouse of spiritual counsel and prayer, interceding for thousands who visited her. She was canonized in 2014 by Pope Francis.

## Early Life and Carmelite Call

Euphrasia was born Rose Eluvathingal on October 17, 1877, in Kattoor, Kerala. From childhood, she showed an intense inclination toward prayer, reciting the rosary frequently and expressing a desire to dedicate her life entirely to God. Despite her father's early resistance, she entered the Congregation of the Mother of Carmel (CMC), founded by Saint Kuriakose Elias Chavara, taking the name Euphrasia of the Sacred Heart.

## A Life of Continuous Prayer

Sister Euphrasia was known for her extraordinary devotion to the Blessed Sacrament and the Sacred Heart of Jesus. She spent hours in front of the tabernacle, earning her the title of "the Praying Mother." She served as novice mistress and later as mother superior at St. Mary's Convent in Ollur. Her spiritual letters reveal a soul deeply immersed in mystical prayer, finding profound peace in silence and contemplation.

## Legacy and Canonization

Mother Euphrasia lived to the age of 74, passing away peacefully on August 29, 1952, at Ollur. Immediately upon her death, her grave became a place of prayer and miracles. She was beatified on December 3, 2006, by Cardinal Varkey Vithayathil on behalf of Pope Benedict XVI, and canonized on November 23, 2014, by Pope Francis.
